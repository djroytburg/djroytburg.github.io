I'm excited to launch this blog as a space to share thoughts, insights, and reflections on AI safety, ethics, and the intersection of technical and normative approaches to building better AI systems.

# Why This Blog?

Throughout my research, I've encountered fascinating questions that don't always fit neatly into academic papers. This blog will serve as a home for different types of content.

## Work in Progress

I'll share **work-in-progress ideas** that are still taking shape, alongside reflections on recent papers in AI safety and ethics.

### Commentary and Technical Dives

You'll also find commentary on current events in AI policy and governance, as well as technical deep dives into methods and approaches.

## What to Expect

I plan to write about topics at the intersection of my research interests: language models, social computing, interpretability, and the ways technical safety work can (and should) engage with normative ethics.

Some posts will be technical, diving into experimental results or methodological approaches. Others will be more conceptual, exploring the philosophical and ethical dimensions of AI development.

# A Note on Cross-Disciplinary Work

One theme you'll see throughout this blog is my conviction that AI safety and AI ethics need to be in conversation with each other. As my recent work on [bridging these fields](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.10058) shows, the separation between technical and normative approaches is both artificial and counterproductive.

I hope this space can contribute, in some small way, to fostering that conversation.
